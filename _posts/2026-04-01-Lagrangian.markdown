I wrote this in February 2026. I was mainly trying to understand a few lines in the derivation shown in Landau & Lifshitz's *Mechanics*. The crucial bits are understading where the first variation comes from, why it must be 0 at an extremum of the action, and then applying integration by parts to make it look like the Euler Lagrange Equations we all know and love. Another book, *An Introduction to the Calculus of Variations* (Fox, 1950) was also helpful to me when I was figuring this out.
